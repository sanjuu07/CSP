The community service report shall have only a one-page executive summary. It shall include a brief description of the Community and summary of all the activities done by the student in CSP and five or more learning objectives and outcomes.

Horticulture is the science and art of cultivating plants for food, medicinal purposes, ornamental use, and environmental sustainability. It encompasses a wide range of activities, including plant cultivation, crop production, landscaping, and plant breeding. The horticulture industry plays a significant role in global food security, economic development, and environmental conservation.

Activities:

1. Visited the village and identified the problems of people due to lack of soil fertility.
2. Conducted a survey on crop production.
3. Engaged with community members to gather their perspectives and insights.
4. Collaborated with local authorities and organizations to gather data and information on internet.
5. Analyzed survey results and compiled a comprehensive report on the cultivated crop

Learning Objectives and Outcomes:

Knowledge of Plant Biology: Understand the basic principles of plant biology, including plant structure, growth processes, reproduction, and physiology. Gain knowledge of plant classification, taxonomy, and identification.

Crop Selection and Cultivation: Acquire knowledge of different horticultural crops, their growth requirements, and cultivation techniques. Learn how to select appropriate crops based on factors like climate, soil conditions, market demand, and cultural practices.

Soil and Nutrient Management: Learn about soil properties, soil fertility, and nutrient requirements for horticultural crops. Understand the methods and practices involved in soil testing, soil amendment, and fertilization to optimize plant growth and productivity.

Pest and Disease Management: Gain an understanding of common pests, diseases, and weeds affecting horticultural crops. The integrated pest management (IPM) strategies, including biological controls, cultural practices, and judicious pesticide use to minimize crop damage.
